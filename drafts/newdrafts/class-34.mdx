<QuestionBlock type="reading-content">
<Question level={1}>Book X.6-8 closes the Ethics in the way Book I opened, with a discussion of the good life.</Question>
<Question level={2}>Is book X's conclusions about the good life consistent with Book I (and the things we've learned along the way)?</Question>
</QuestionBlock>

<QuestionBlock type="reading-content">
<Question level={1}>Just for the sake of review, note that in chapter 6, he states "happiness is not a state/disposition".</Question>
<Question level={2}>Can you understand why he makes this distinction? Is this consistent with what we've read so far? (Another way of asking the question would be: why are happiness and virtue not identical? What is different about them?)</Question>
</QuestionBlock>

<QuestionBlock type="reading-content">
<Question level={1}>What's the difference between a virtuous activity and a "pleasant amusement" (c. 6)?</Question>
</QuestionBlock>

<QuestionBlock type="reading-content">
<Question level={1}>What does Aristotle recognize as the "highest activity" (c. 7)?</Question>
<Question level={2}>Are there any reasons given to support this conclusion? (There are at least six.)</Question>
</QuestionBlock>

<QuestionBlock type="reading-content">
<Question level={1}>How does this life differ from the life lived according to "practical virtues" (c. 7, near 1177a22)?</Question>
</QuestionBlock>

<QuestionBlock type="reading-content">
<Question level={1}>Why does he say that the contemplative life is "too high for man"?</Question>
<Question level={2}>Is it odd that he would still urge us to pursue this life?</Question>
</QuestionBlock>

<QuestionBlock type="reading-content">
<Question level={1}>A few more differences between the "two lives" are discussed in c. 8.</Question>
<Question level={2}>How do these two lives differ in terms of needing external goods?</Question>
</QuestionBlock>

<QuestionBlock type="reading-discussion">
<Question level={1}>In chapter 7, why does Aristotle state that the life of contemplation is the most self-sufficient life (see in particular pp. 440-441)? </Question>
<Question level={2}>Why does he say it is more self-sufficient than the life of the politician or the warrior?</Question>
<Question level={2}>Can you think of an example to illustrate the difference he is trying to describe?</Question>
</QuestionBlock>
