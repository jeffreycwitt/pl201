Class 15 Guided Reading Questions

Heraclitus of Ephesus, pp. 39-54

show participation instructions

What do you think Heraclitus means by "logos" (Consider especially fragments 1, 2, 10, 11, 27)?
How are the images of "appearance" versus "reality, "waking" versus "dreaming" used in these fragments?
Which fragments discuss these ideas?
Why do you think Heraclitus seems preoccupied with the difference between "appearance" and "reality"?
Why is Heraclitus fascinated with the image of fire? (See esp. fragment 49.)
Is it similar or different to the theories of Thales, Anaximander, or Anaximenes? How so?
Why does he associate it with the underlying "matter" of the world?
How is fire related to the frequently discussed ideas of change and rest? (See fragment 39, 40, 41, 55, 79, 81, 82.)
What is Heraclitus's general attitude toward other people in these fragments?
Which fragments appear to you as suggestive of this attitude?
In what way does Heraclitus's account of the world as one of constant change appear to you correct or incorrect, in agreement with your own experience or opposed to your own experience?
Explain where and how you have had these experiences and why they confirm or contradict Heraclitus's own view of the world as constantly in flux.
