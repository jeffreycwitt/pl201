Class 33 Guided Reading Questions

Plato’s Gorgias 461b-472c, pp. 27-45

show participation instructions

Where does Polus thinks Gorgias has gone wrong? What is he prepared to admit that Gorgias was not (p. 27)?
Be familiar with the four kinds of flattery.
What are the four corresponding types of expertise?
How is flattery distinguished from expertise (see pp. 30-33)?
On p. 35, Socrates asserts a position that is shocking to Polus. He then attempts to prove it by asserting a paradox.
What is this paradox (see bottom of p. 35)?
How does Socrates prove this paradox using the distinction between “doing what he thinks is best” and “doing what he wants”?
Despite the objections of Polus, why does Socrates think the unjust dictator is the least powerful person? What else does Socrates think is required for true power and why? (See esp. pp. 34-35.)
Compare this to one example of injustice or attempt at domination that we see today.
Why might Socrates see such an act of injustice as an example “impotence” rather than “real power”?
