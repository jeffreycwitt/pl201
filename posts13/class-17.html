Class 17 Guided Reading Questions

Anaxagoras, pp. 101-108

show participation instructions

As always, please be sure to read the biographical introduction to each of our thinkers.

Why does Anaxagoras repeatedly say: "all things are mixed together" or "all things are together"?
What are the "things" he is talking about?
How does this allow him to agree with Parmenides that "nothing every comes into or goes out of existence"?
What does Anaxagoras say about the concept of mind (nous)? What is its function or purpose?
Does it have any similarity to Heraclitus's idea of Logos?
In what ways do you see Anaxagoras trying to solve the problem or paradox introduced by Parmenides?
If Parmenides claims that change is impossible, in what ways do you see Anaxagoras trying to both accept this conclusion and at the same time account for our daily experience of change?
