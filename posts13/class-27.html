Class 27 Guided Reading Questions

Aristotle, N. Ethics, Book I, cc. 1-10, pp. 347-363

show participation instructions

In chapter one, Aristotle identifies two kinds of activities? What are they and how do they relate to one another?
In the same chapter, what does Aristotle mean by "the good"?
How is the idea of “the good” related to the notion of “happiness” (see c. 4)?
In chapter 4-5, Aristotle considers at least four things that people identify with happiness. What are they? Why are they found wanting?
Why is “self-sufficiency” such an important part of the notion of happiness (c. 7)
How does Aristotle begin his own search for human happiness (see. c. 7)?
What does he look to first? To what kind of "cause" does he appeal? (Consider in particular his attention the human "function")
Where do we look when considering the “good” of a particular art or craft?
How does Aristotle arrive at his description of the function of human beings?
How and why are reason a part of this definition?
What is this final formulation?
Can there be more than one activity that the happy man performs?
Why does he add the criteria of "in a complete life"?
What "common beliefs" fit with the theoretical answer Aristotle has produced (see c. 8)?
Does his definition of happiness include virtue?
Which is more important, the possession or use of virtue? Why?
What do you think of Aristotle's definition of the function of a human being introduced in chapter 7?
Is this a plausible definition to you? Why or why not?
