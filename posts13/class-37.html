Class 37 Guided Reading Questions

Plato’s Gorgias, 506c-513c, pp. 102-135

show participation instructions

How do the virtues get redefined after Socrates argues for the distinction between pleasure and the good?
Temperance (pp. 102-103)
Justice (see p. 105)
Courage (see p. 105)
What is the role for cleverness/expertise/wisdom now? How is this different than the reason Callicles valued cleverness?
According to Socrates, pp. 109-110, why is it difficult to protect yourself from both committing injustice and suffering injustice?
Why, in the end, must we choose to focus on one form of protection at the cost of the other?
On page 107, Socrates finally responds to Callicles’ earlier claim that Socrates is pathetic because he is not able to protect himself.
How does Socrates respond?
Why does Socrates think it is actually Callicles, rather than he, who is not able to protect himself?
In the final section pp. 126-135, Socrates appeals to his belief in the afterlife.
How important do you think this belief is to his overall claim that "it is better to suffer injustice than to commit injustice"?
Do you think this claim would remain true even if the after-life that Socrates describes did not exist.
