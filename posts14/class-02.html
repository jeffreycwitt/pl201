Class 2 Reading Questions

Josef Pieper, Leisure as the Basis of Culture, pp. 19-52

show participation instructions

What objection does Pieper propose at the beginning of his lecture?
Why might we expect to hear this objection today? What is similar about today's cultural climate and the "post WWII" climate in which Pieper is writing?
What is the Greek word for leisure? In what way can school be considered a "leisure activity"?
What does Pieper think is the current cultural attitude toward leisure?
Do you think this attitude persists today?
What images come to mind when you think about leisure?
To what degree do you value leisure time? Is it more or less important than work time?
What are the three principal characteristics of the "Worker Type" mentioned by Pieper (part III)?
How and where does he describe these characteristics in section II?
Why is leisure "alien" and unappealing to such a person?
What is Pieper's critique of these characteristics?
Why are idleness and restlessness connected according to Pieper?
How does idleness/restlessness differ from leisure?
What are the three descriptions of leisure given?
How do these three descriptions compare or challenge the three characteristics of work?
What do you think Pieper means when he says: "In leisure...the truly human is rescued and preserved precisely because the area of the 'just human' is left behind over and over again" (p. 51)?
For example: In what way is the human being impoverished if she is not allowed (or does not allow herself) true leisure? What kind of activities does Pieper seem to associate with “true leisure”?
In what way do agree or disagree?
