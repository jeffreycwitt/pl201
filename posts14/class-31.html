Class 30 Guided Reading Questions

Aristotle, N. Ethics, VIII, cc. 1-3, 9 (417-422) and IX, cc. 4, 7-9, 12 (423-432)

show participation instructions

What are the three types of friendships described by Aristotle (VIII.1-3)?
What characteristics of perfect (or complete) friendship does he list?
What is the problem/puzzle described at the beginning of IX.8?
Do you think it is possible to pursue your own happiness (egoism) and the happiness of a friend (altruism) at the same time? Or does one pursuit always negate the other?
For example, if you do something good for your friend because it makes you happy, are you really being pursuing the good for them (altruism) or are you still just pursuing the good for yourself above all else (egoism)?
Can you think of any examples that support your position?
How does Aristotle distinguish between two senses of self-love to solve the problem introduced in IX.8?
How does the person with proper self-love benefit himself and others at the same time?
